WalkMe

walkme is a safety-enhanced pedestrian routing application. it recommends the safest path from A to B based on recorded coordinate-specific crime data from NYC city data. walkme offers a unique solution to situations when you might be walking alone in an unfamiliar city and would rather optimize for safety rather than distance. major features include displaying route options ranked by a safety index from 1 to 10, which is calculated using a custom heuristic, as well as infographic popups for each route with more statistics that give context for what a certain safety index really means.

we used the Ionic framework, built on top of Angular JS, for multi-platform support. we used a Django backend with a PostgreSQL database. my focus was on the backend and heuristic design.

check out the demo. check out the app.

CoPilot

copilot is a chrome extension that enables groups to browse collaboratively on the web. co-browsing is a new paradigm for group collaboration and productivity - users can create their own shared browsing sessions with others and collaboratively browse the web. imagine being able to plan a trip or research a group project without having to send lists of links to each out. shared browsing sessions occur in a separate chrome window, in which all tabs are mirrored between all participants. actions are mirrored as well.

we built this using a firebase backend to meet the need for a real-time, synchronized database that will keep all clients' tabs matched up. my focus here was writing javascript for the chrome extension. we will be revamping this 24hr-hackathon project soon.

check out the chrome extension.

Pigeon

pigeon is an email that helps organizations (like campuses and companies) send targeted, interest-based emails to members. instead of using listservs or other static mailing lists, pigeon allows users to send any email to only the people who might be interested in the content of the email. this is done through manual and automatic content tagging. it keeps your inbox free of cluttered advertising messages you don't care about and allows you to target your messages to the specific people who will care.

we are using Node.js and a PostgreSQL database. we are also building a chrome extension that uses an open source gmail js library, which, when enabled, will allow users to access pigeon's functionality from their own gmail inbox. my focus is on the backend/database and the chrome extension.

this project is a work in progress. check out the proposal. check back later to see the app.

Clutch

clutch is a mobile retail couponing solution. physical retail offers customers the unique experience of browsing and trying on products before buying, but cannot take advantage of customized suggestions and dynamic pricing on a large scale. while the top e-commerce brands can do that online, they cannot recreate the in-person experience. clutch combines the best of both worlds - it allows physical retailers to offer hyper-local, custom coupons to customers by using iBeacon location technology. for customers, clutch is a more personal shopping experience; for retailers, clutch means more direct access to likely buyers.

we built a simple prototype using Ionic.js and a Parse backend. we also came up with a business model, financial projections, and conducted user surveys to inform our design of this product. my focus was on the business planning.

check out the product page. check out our pitch.

uShare

uShare brings the sharing economy to physical storage. it connects people who need to store stuff temporarily to people who want to use that that stuff. it is the AirBnB for unboxable items. uShare allows storers to bypass the high prices of storage while renters get access to much lower prices. uShare harnesses the unrealized value sitting in storage units everywhere.

after becoming finalists at Tigerlaunch and placing 2nd at the CampusKudos pitch competition, we launched a prototype at Princeton University for the bike storage vertical during the summer.

check out our product page. check out our pitch. check out this news article.
